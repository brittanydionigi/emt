Meds

  • Albuterol - carried on EMS Unit
    • Indications: bronchospasm, prescribed a rescue inhaler
    • Contra-Indications: cardiac disorder (eschemic cardiac disease, angina, previous heart attack, bypass, stent)
    • Dosages: 2.5mg albuterol in 3cc saline via nebulizer at 6 l/m (6cc saline for 6mos-2yrs)
    • Always call med control to administer 2nd dose
  • Nitro - assisted administration
    • Indications: chest pain, has a prescription for nitro
    • Contra-Indications: systolic blood pressure < 90, 50 > heart rate > 100, suspected head injury, ED medication, max dose exceeded
    • Dosages: 0.3mg per tablet every 3-5 minutes, max of 3 doses in 24hrs
  • Baby Aspirin - carried on EMS unit
    • Indications: chest discomfort suggestive of heart attack
    • Contra-Indications: allergic to aspirin
    • Dosages: 325mg
    • Administration: instruct patient to chew aspirin
  • Epinephrine - carried on EMS unit
    • Indications: shows signs of severe allergic reaction
    • Contra-Indications: none
    • Dosages: 0.3mg in 1:1000, (0.15mg in 1:2000 < 55lbs)
    • Administration: Intramuscularly into the lateral thigh, holding for 10 seconds & reassess after 5 minutes and repeat if necessary
  • Narcan - carried on EMS unit
    • Indications: apparent opiate OD, RR < 8
    • Contra-Indications:
    • Dosages: 2mg per 2ml, 1mg per nostril
    • Administration: spray up nose
  • Glocuse - carried on EMS unit
    • Indications: low blood sugar
    • Contra-Indications: unresponsive, gag reflex
    • Dosages: 1 packet

Initial Assessment

  • Hi there, how are you doing today? What seems to be the problem?
  • Pt can answer: My patient can speak to me so I have a patent airway
  • I'm looking for work of breathing, chest rise, respiratory rate, are they speaking in full sentences?
  • Dyspnic: We're going to give you some oxygen with a non-rebreather mask
  • Check radial pulse for RRQ
  • Touch skin for color, temperature, quality

Decision

  • The patient is sick we're going to continue our assessment as we package for transport

OPQRST

  • I'm just going to have my partner get a set of vital signs while we figure out what's going on
  • Onset/Time: how long have you been feeling this way? did it come on suddenly? has this ever happened to you before?
  • Events: what were you doing when this came on?
  • Palliative: is there anything that makes this better or worse?
  • Quality: can you describe your pain?
  • Radiation: can you point to a spot where it hurts or is it all over?
  • Severity: on a scale of 1-10 rate the severity of your complaint? how does it compare to previous episodes?

Physical Exam

  • Neuro: pupils, equal grips, orthostatic signs
  • Respiratory: breath sounds, tongue
  • Cardiac: palpate the chest

SAMPLE

  • Signs/Symptoms: verify their chief complaint
  • Allergies: any allergies to medications?
  • Medications: can you tell me what medications you're taking and what they're for? have you been compliant with your meds?
  • Past: any other notable medical conditions, hospitalizations you might have?
  • Last Oral Intake: last time you ate?
  • Events: and you said you were doing ____ when this happened?

Transport
