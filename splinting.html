CSM

  • Exposing the injury site and visualizing the affected leg
  • Checking distal pulses
  • Can you wiggle your toes for me?
  • Which toe am I touching?
  • Push down, push up
  • Partner can you stabilize the leg and hold traction Make sure they are holding the leg properly & there is no movement

Applying traction splint

  • Measuring the traction splint 6-12 inches longer than the unaffected limb Measure the splint
  • Position & prepare the straps: above ankle, below knee, above knee, above injury
  • Slide splint underneath the leg
  • Apply ischial strap loosely, pad the groin and tighten
  • Double-check straps are in the right position
  • Raise the kickstand
  • Apply the ankle hitch to the ankle, S-loophole underneath
  • Pull pin and bring traction strap over the ratchet guard
  • Turn ratchet to take up any slack
  • Fasten straps in appropriate places

Upper Extremity

Equipment:

  • 2 padded boards
  • up to 5 cravats

Fab Five

  • BSI
  • scene safety
  • mechanism of injury
  • # of patients
  • additional resources

CSM

  • Can you wiggle your fingers?
  • Checking radial pulse - which finger am I touching?

Splint injury

  • Clavicle: tie sling UNDER arm so it doesn't go over clavicle injury
  • Elbow: include arm in both ends of boards; modified slip-knot sling; swathe goes over end of board to avoid elbow
  • Humerus: line up lower end of board with elbow
  • Forearm: provide position of function in hand
  • Wrist: provide position of function in hand
  • Tie swathe knots off to the side of unaffected arm
  • Double-cross knots so they don't come loose, tie in bows, pad

CSM

  • Can you wiggle your fingers?
  • Checking radial pulse - which finger am I touching?
